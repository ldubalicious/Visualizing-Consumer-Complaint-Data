The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau is a government agency created in the wake of the 2008 financial crisis to ensure that banks, lenders, and other financial companies treat American consumers fairly.

In July 2011, the CFPB began collecting consumer complaints against financial institutions. Now, the Consumer Complaint Database is a collection of 711,381 complaints, on a range of consumer financial products and services, sent to nearly 3,000 companies for response.

The data shown here is pulled from the CFPB's Consumer Complaint Database API and the New York Times API.
